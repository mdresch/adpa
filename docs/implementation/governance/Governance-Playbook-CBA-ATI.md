# Implementation Governance Playbook
*Client:* **CBA** · *Program:* **Assurance Transformation Initiative (ATI)**

## 1. Project Organization & Leadership Structure
### 1.1 Governance Philosophy
The Assurance Transformation Initiative (ATI) will be governed by a hierarchical structure emphasizing clear accountability, decisive leadership, and efficient decision-making. This structure promotes proactive risk management, seamless communication, and transparency across all project phases. Authority is delegated based on expertise and responsibility. The Steering Committee provides strategic direction and oversight, ensuring alignment with CBA's Assurance strategy and objectives. Progress will be tracked rigorously, and deviations from the plan will be addressed promptly and transparently, as outlined in Governance-Ch1-Leadership.

### 1.2 Steering Committee Charter
- Membership: Chief Assurance Officer (CAO), Chief Information Officer (CIO), Chief Financial Officer (CFO), Head of Audit, Head of Sustainability, Menno Drescher (Project Manager).
- Cadence: Monthly, with ad-hoc meetings as required for critical decisions.

# Governance Playbook - Master

## 1. Executive Summary
The Assurance Transformation Initiative (ATI) is a strategic project under CBA's Assurance business, designed to modernize audit methodologies by integrating AI-driven analytics, blockchain verification, and real-time sustainability reporting. This initiative enhances audit accuracy, reduces manual effort, and delivers real-time insights to clients. By leveraging cutting-edge technologies, ATI ensures CBA remains competitive in the evolving corporate reporting landscape. Key objectives include technology integration, enhanced data analytics, and improved reporting capabilities. The initiative directly supports CBA’s goal of fostering trust in the global economy through innovative and high-quality assurance services. The project has a Phase 1 budget of $2.8M and an 18-month timeline (Pilot: 6 months, Full Rollout: 12 months), as detailed in the Business Case Template. Benefits include improved audit efficiency, enhanced client satisfaction, and increased market share.

## 4. Change Control
All changes to the project scope, schedule, or budget must go through a formal change control process. The Change Control Board (CCB) is responsible for reviewing and approving change requests.

### 4.1 Change Control Process
The change control process consists of the following steps:
1.  **Identify the Need for Change:** The need for a change is identified by any project stakeholder.
2.  **Submit Change Request:** A formal change request is submitted to the project manager, including a description of the proposed change, the rationale for the change, and the potential impact on the project.
3.  **Assess Impact:** The project manager assesses the impact of the proposed change on the project scope, schedule, budget, and resources.
4.  **Review by CCB:** The Change Control Board (CCB) reviews the change request and the impact assessment. See Governance-Ch1-Leadership for further info.
5.  **Decision:** The CCB makes a decision to approve, reject, or defer the change request.
6.  **Implement Change:** If the change request is approved, the project team implements the change.
7.  **Update Project Documentation:** The project manager updates the project documentation to reflect the change.
