**Purpose and Business Value** *(PMBOK 8 Principle: Value Delivery)*:

Project Phoenix exists to dismantle a costly, fragmented, and underperforming analytics ecosystem built on Tableau and replace it with a unified, cloud-native Microsoft Azure Synapse and Power BI Premium platform. This strategic migration directly addresses critical business challenges, including unsustainable Total Cost of Ownership (TCO), siloed data inhibiting cross-functional insights, and a technical inability to scale self-service analytics to meet growing business demand.

The **primary value** delivered is a projected **38% reduction in TCO, realizing $4.2 million in annual run-rate savings** by FY2027. This is achieved by eliminating redundant licensing, reducing infrastructure maintenance overhead, and consolidating 62 disparate data sources. Further value is created by empowering over 500 business users with faster, more accessible data through **sub-3-second dashboard load times** and achieving **90% active user adoption**. From a **systems thinking** perspective, this project is not merely a technology swap; it is a foundational enabler for the organization's broader digital transformation strategy, creating a scalable "single source of truth" that will fuel future data-driven decision-making across Sales, Marketing, Operations, and Finance.

## 2. Development Approach and Tailoring Strategy
*(PMBOK 8 Performance Domain: Development Approach & Life Cycle)*

### 2.1 Selected Development Approach

**Approach**: Hybrid

**Rationale** *(PMBOK 8 Principle: Adaptability)*:
A Hybrid approach has been selected to balance the need for agile, iterative development with the requirement for robust, predictive governance and financial control. This model leverages the strengths of both methodologies to manage the project's unique characteristics effectively.

- **Uncertainty Level**: Requirements for individual report migrations and user interface design have moderate uncertainty, making an Agile (Scrum) approach ideal for the development teams to iterate based on user feedback. However, the overall scope, budget, and infrastructure deployment are well-defined, suiting a Predictive approach for high-level planning.
- **Stakeholder Availability**: High engagement from business users and departmental power users is critical for report validation and UAT. Agile sprints with regular demos and feedback sessions will maximize this engagement.
- **Compliance Constraints**: Financial reporting and data governance requirements necessitate formal stage-gate reviews and approvals, which are best managed within a Predictive framework wrapper.
- **Delivery Cadence**: The project will deliver value incrementally through quarterly Program Increments (PIs), with smaller, feature-complete deliverables released at the end of each 3-week sprint.

This tailored approach consists of 3-week Agile sprints for the development and migration of Power BI reports, wrapped within three major 3-month predictive stage-gates (phases) for infrastructure deployment, major migration waves, and final decommissioning.

---

### 2.2 Tailoring Justification

**Why This Approach Was Selected** *(PMBOK 8 Domains)*:

| Performance Domain | Tailoring Decision | Justification |
|--------------------|-------------------|---------------|
| **Planning** | Rolling-wave planning with predictive stage-gates | Balances the need for a high-level, 9-month roadmap for sponsors with the flexibility of detailed sprint planning for the development team, accommodating evolving report requirements. |
| **Delivery** | Incremental delivery of report bundles | Delivers tangible value to business units early and often. This allows for early feedback, reduces risk, and builds momentum by demonstrating progress. |
| **Measurement** | Dual-level metrics: Sprint Velocity & EVM | Agile metrics (Velocity, Burndown) track team-level performance, while Earned Value Management (EVM) at the stage-gate level provides executive-level insight into schedule and cost performance (SPI/CPI). |
| **Uncertainty** | Risk-based prototyping for complex reports | For the top 10% of complex Tableau workbooks, ambiguity in logic will be addressed via rapid prototypes in Power BI early in the process to de-risk migration efforts and confirm user requirements. |

**Tailoring Applied**:
- A modified Scrum framework will be used for report development teams (3-week sprints).
- A stage-gate model will govern the three main project phases: 1) Infrastructure & Pilot, 2) Mass Migration, and 3) Decommissioning & Optimization.
- The Change Control Board (CCB) will manage any changes affecting the approved scope, schedule, or cost baselines, integrating predictive control into the agile workflow.
