1. Executive Summary

The "CBA Body of Knowledge Guide Development" project, led by Project Manager Menno Drescher and sponsored by Dr. Eleanor Vance (Executive Sponsor), aims to create a definitive, comprehensive guide for the Certified Business Analyst (CBA) certification program. This initiative directly addresses existing inconsistent practices and knowledge gaps among CBA professionals by providing a standardized, current, and authoritative reference. The project seeks to significantly enhance the program's credibility, elevate business analysis skills, and ultimately increase the market value and competitiveness of CBA professionals globally.

Scheduled from July 1 to December 31, 2024, with an estimated budget of $250,000, the project embraces an adaptive/hybrid project management approach, aligning with PMBOK 7's emphasis on value delivery. Key objectives include finalizing Version 1.0 of the guide, achieving 90% positive feedback from a pilot review group, establishing digital-first distribution, and maintaining high stakeholder alignment throughout the development lifecycle. The guide will differentiate itself through practical, future-ready content (e.g., AI integration, business agility), interactive features, and a community-driven update model, ensuring unparalleled relevance and actionable value.

2.1. Project Purpose and Business Justification

The project's primary purpose is to establish a standardized, comprehensive, and up-to-date guide for the Certified Business Analyst (CBA) certification program, serving as the authoritative reference for business analysis principles and practices.

Business Justification: The existing CBA program currently lacks a single, standardized, and updated guide, which leads to:

- Inconsistent practices among certified professionals.
- Knowledge gaps in contemporary business analysis techniques.
- Potential erosion of the CBA certification's value and credibility.

Developing this guide will:

- Standardize Practice: Provide a common lexicon, best practices, and methodologies across the CBA community.
- Enhance Credibility: Elevate the rigor, relevance, and value of the CBA certification.
- Promote Professional Development: Equip professionals with future-ready skills, including adaptive methodologies, AI integration, and data analytics.
- Drive Value: Improve business analysis effectiveness, leading to better project outcomes and organizational efficiency.
- Strategic Positioning: Cement the organization as a thought leader in the business analysis domain. This project is grounded in PMBOK 7's Value Delivery principle, ensuring tangible benefits for all stakeholders.

6. Communicate Decision: Project Manager communicates the CCB's decision to all relevant stakeholders.

8. Key User Stories for CBA Body of Knowledge Guide

These user stories capture the functional and non-functional requirements from the perspective of our primary users, driving value delivery in an adaptive approach.

8.1. Story 1: Core Knowledge Access

User Story: As a CBA candidate, I want to easily navigate and search for core business analysis knowledge areas, so that I can quickly find the information needed for my certification exam and daily practice.

Acceptance Criteria:

- Given I am on the digital guide platform, when I search for "Requirements Elicitation," then I see relevant sections and definitions.
- When I click on a knowledge area (e.g., "Strategy Analysis"), then I am presented with its sub-sections and key concepts in a clear, structured format.
- When I am offline, then I can still access downloaded core content modules.

8.2. Story 2: Practical Application & Tools

User Story: As a certified Business Analyst, I want to access real-world case studies and downloadable templates within the guide, so that I can apply learned techniques directly to my projects and enhance my productivity.

Acceptance Criteria:

- Given I am viewing a specific technique (e.g., "Stakeholder Analysis"), when I scroll down, then I see at least two practical examples or mini-case studies demonstrating its application.
- When a template (e.g., "Business Process Model") is mentioned, then I can download a customizable version directly from the platform.
- When I complete a case study, then I can compare my approach to an expert-recommended solution or best practice.

8.3. Story 3: Adaptive Learning & Updates

User Story: As a CBA professional, I want to receive regular, modular updates on emerging trends and new practices (e.g., AI in BA, Agile Business Analysis), so that my knowledge remains current and relevant in a rapidly evolving industry.

Acceptance Criteria:

- Given a significant industry change occurs (e.g., new AI tool for requirements), when I log into the platform, then I receive a notification of new or updated content modules related to that change.
- When I access an updated module, then I can clearly see what content has been added or revised (e.g., via versioning or highlights).
- When I provide feedback on a module, then I receive an acknowledgment and potential follow-up from the content team.
