# Workshop Participation Target Breakdown: 10,000 Attendees in 12 Months

**Project:** Microsoft Experience Centers Amsterdam  
**Target:** 10,000 workshop attendees in the first 12 months  
**Date:** 2026-01-24  
**Context:** Retail/in-shop workshop participation for customers and signed-up customers

---

## Executive Summary

To achieve **10,000 workshop attendees** in 12 months, the Experience Center must deliver approximately **833 attendees per month**, **192 per week**, or **27 per day** (assuming 7-day operation). This requires a strategic mix of **walk-in workshops** (for retail customers) and **pre-registered workshops** (for signed-up customers), with variable durations to accommodate different customer needs and engagement levels.

## Retail/In-Shop Workshop Participation Model

### Walk-In Workshops (Retail Customers)

**Characteristics:**
- **Duration:** 15-45 minutes
- **Capacity:** 8-15 people
- **Frequency:** High (every 30-60 minutes)
- **Target:** 40-50% of total attendees (~4,000-5,000/year)

**Examples:**
- "5-Minute Azure Demo" (15 min, 10 people, every hour)
- "Surface Device Hands-On" (30 min, 12 people, 6/day)
- "AI Copilot Quick Start" (45 min, 15 people, 4/day)

**Scheduling:**
- **Peak Hours (10 AM - 2 PM, 4 PM - 7 PM):** Every 30-45 minutes
- **Off-Peak Hours:** Every 60-90 minutes
- **Weekends:** Higher frequency (every 30 minutes during peak)

---

### Pre-Registered Workshops (Signed-Up Customers)

**Characteristics:**
- **Duration:** 60-180 minutes
- **Capacity:** 8-12 people
- **Frequency:** Moderate (2-4 per day)
- **Target:** 50-60% of total attendees (~5,000-6,000/year)

**Examples:**
- "Azure Fundamentals Workshop" (90 min, 12 people, 2/day)
- "AI Development Lab" (120 min, 10 people, 1/day)
- "Digital Transformation Masterclass" (180 min, 8 people, 1/day)

**Scheduling:**
- **Advance Booking:** 1-2 weeks in advance
- **Time Slots:** 10 AM, 2 PM, 6 PM (weekdays), 11 AM, 3 PM (weekends)
- **Waitlist Management:** For oversubscribed sessions

- **Walk-ins (45%):** Capture spontaneous interest from retail visitors
- **Pre-registered (55%):** Ensure committed participation and better planning

## Key Success Factors

### 1. **Flexible Scheduling**
- **Quick workshops** every 30-60 minutes during peak hours
- **Standard workshops** 3-4 times per day
- **Deep dive workshops** 1-2 times per day
- **Weekend focus** on family-friendly and beginner workshops

### 2. **Dual Participation Model**
- **Walk-in slots:** Reserve 40-50% capacity for spontaneous visitors
- **Pre-registration:** Require advance booking for longer workshops
- **Hybrid approach:** Allow walk-ins to join if space available

### 3. **Capacity Management**
- **Overbooking strategy:** Allow 10-15% overbooking for no-shows
- **Waitlist system:** For popular workshops
- **Standby list:** For walk-ins when pre-registered attendees don't show

### 4. **Marketing & Awareness**
- **In-store signage:** Promote daily workshop schedule
- **Digital displays:** Real-time availability and next session info
- **Mobile app:** Pre-registration and reminders
- **Email campaigns:** Weekly workshop calendar to signed-up customers

### 5. **Staffing Requirements**
- **Workshop facilitators:** 2-3 full-time equivalents
- **Support staff:** 1-2 for registration and setup
- **Peak coverage:** Additional part-time facilitators during weekends

## Risk Mitigation

### Risk 1: Low Walk-In Participation
**Mitigation:**
- Aggressive in-store promotion
- Incentives (discounts, freebies) for workshop participation
- Gamification (workshop badges, loyalty points)

### Risk 2: High No-Show Rate
**Mitigation:**
- Overbooking by 10-15%
- Reminder system (SMS/email 24 hours before)
- Standby list for walk-ins
- Cancellation policy (require 24-hour notice)

### Risk 3: Seasonal Variations
**Mitigation:**
- Adjust capacity during peak seasons (holidays, summer)
- Special event workshops during slow periods
- Partner with local tech communities for group bookings

### Risk 4: Workshop Room Availability
**Mitigation:**
- Flexible room configurations
- Multi-purpose spaces
- External venue partnerships for overflow

## Conclusion

Achieving **10,000 workshop attendees** in 12 months requires:

1. **Daily Target:** 27-38 attendees per day
2. **Workshop Mix:** Combination of quick demos (15-30 min) and standard workshops (60-120 min)
3. **Dual Model:** 45% walk-ins, 55% pre-registered
4. **Capacity:** 2-3 workshop rooms with flexible scheduling
5. **Frequency:** 8-12 workshop sessions per day across all rooms

With proper capacity planning, flexible scheduling, and a mix of walk-in and pre-registered workshops, the target is **achievable and sustainable**. The recommended balanced model provides **5x capacity** above target, allowing for seasonal variations, no-shows, and growth opportunities.
