## 1. Executive Summary

This plan defines the processes for requirements elicitation, analysis, documentation, and validation for the ADPA AI Driven Risk Management initiative.

**Key Objectives**:
- Reduce project disruptions by 40%
- Improve issue resolution time by 30%
- Ensure compliance with ISO/IEC 23894, NIST AI RMF, EU AI Act

---

## 2. Scope

### In-Scope
- AI-Driven Risk Management
- Automated Issue Escalation
- AI-Powered Issue Management
- Resolution Guidance
- Compliance with governance frameworks

### Out-of-Scope
- Replacement of existing project management tools
- Full automation of risk mitigation
- AI-driven resource allocation (future phase)

## 4. Analysis Approach

### 4.1 Elicitation Techniques

| Technique | Purpose | Stakeholders | Output |
|-----------|---------|--------------|--------|
| Workshops | Validate requirements | PMs, SMEs, AI Specialists | Requirements documents |
| Interviews | Gather detailed needs | CIO, CFO, Compliance | User stories, use cases |
| Document Analysis | Review existing processes | BA, Data Scientists | Process models, data flows |
| Surveys | Collect broad feedback | End Users | Pain points, preferences |

### 4.2 Requirements Taxonomy

| Type | Description | Example |
|------|-------------|---------|
| Business | High-level organizational needs | "Reduce disruptions by 40%" |
| Stakeholder | Needs of specific stakeholders | "PMs need real-time risk alerts" |
| Functional | System capabilities | "AI predicts risks with 90% accuracy" |
| Non-Functional | Quality attributes | "Comply with EU AI Act" |
| Constraints | Limitations | "Budget: €5M", "Timeline: 18 months" |

|--------|-------------|
| Business Value | Maximize ROI through AI efficiencies |

### 6.2 Option Evaluation

| Option | Description | Pros | Cons |
|--------|-------------|------|------|
| Build In-House | Develop AI internally | Full control | High cost, long timeline |
| Buy COTS | Purchase off-the-shelf | Faster, lower cost | Limited customization |
| Hybrid | Combine both | Balanced approach | Integration complexity |

**Recommended**: Hybrid approach
