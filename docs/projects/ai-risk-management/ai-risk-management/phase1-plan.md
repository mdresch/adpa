# AI Risk Assessment Resolutions 2026 – Phase 1 Plan

## 1. Purpose and Scope
- **Purpose**: Establish foundational AI risk governance, assessment methods, and documentation so the organization can safely evaluate, procure, and deploy AI solutions from 2026 onward.
- **Scope**: All AI-related systems, services, vendors, and pilot initiatives, including generative AI, predictive models, embedded third-party AI features, and internal automation using machine learning.

## 2. Objectives (Phase 1 – Q1 2026)
1. Define and approve AI governance and decision-making structures.
2. Create a complete inventory of current and planned AI use cases.
3. Standardize AI risk taxonomy, assessment methodology, and risk register.
4. Establish baseline data, privacy, and security controls for AI.
5. Define third-party AI vendor evaluation and onboarding standards.
6. Publish initial AI policies and usage guidance for the organization.

## 3. Governance and Roles
- **Executive Sponsor**: \[Name, role]
- **AI Risk Steering Committee**:
  - Members: Risk, Compliance, Security, Legal, Data, IT, Business.
  - Responsibilities: Approve high-risk use cases, accept residual risk, prioritize remediation.
- **Model Owner**: Accountable for each AI use case’s lifecycle and risk posture.
- **Risk & Compliance**: Own methodology, challenge assessments, maintain risk register.
- **Security**: Own technical controls, security testing, incident response integration.
- **Data Owner**: Approve data usage and ensure compliance with privacy rules.

## 4. Risk Taxonomy (High-Level)
Categories include, but are not limited to:
- Data & Privacy risk  
- Security & Abuse risk  
- Bias & Fairness risk  
- Explainability & Transparency risk  
- Robustness & Performance drift  
- Legal & IP risk  
- Operational & Resilience risk  
- Reputational & Ethical risk  
- Third-Party & Supply Chain risk  

Detailed definitions can be captured in an **AI Risk Taxonomy** appendix for Phase 2.

## 5. Assessment Methodology
- **Step 1 – Identify**
  - Describe use case, data, users impacted, model type, deployment context, and dependency on third parties.
- **Step 2 – Inherent Risk Rating**
  - Likelihood: 1–5 (Very Low to Very High).
  - Impact: 1–5 (Very Low to Very High).
  - Inherent Risk Score = Likelihood × Impact.
- **Step 3 – Control Assessment**
  - Evaluate control design and effectiveness per risk category (Strong / Adequate / Weak / None).
- **Step 4 – Residual Risk**
  - Residual Risk Score = Inherent Risk Score – Control Effectiveness Adjustment.
  - Map to qualitative rating: Low / Moderate / High / Critical.
- **Step 5 – Decision and Documentation**
  - Accept, mitigate, transfer, or avoid.
  - Record in the AI Risk Register with owner and target dates.

## 6. Phase 1 Deliverables
- AI Governance Charter.
- AI Risk Appetite Statement.
- AI Use Case Inventory & Criticality Matrix.
- AI Risk Register (initial population).
- AI Data Readiness & Quality Assessment template.
- AI Vendor Due Diligence Questionnaire and scoring model.
- Enterprise AI Policy and AI Development Standard (baseline versions).

## 7. Timeline (Q1 2026)
- **Week 1–2**: Confirm sponsors, approve this plan, stand up Steering Committee.
- **Week 2–4**: Build use case inventory; draft governance charter and risk taxonomy.
- **Week 4–6**: Finalize risk methodology, templates, and data/privacy baseline controls.
- **Week 6–10**: Populate risk register for priority use cases; perform vendor due diligence.
- **Week 10–12**: Approve policies, finalize documentation, and hand over to BAU governance.

## 8. Success Metrics
- 100% of identified AI use cases inventoried with a criticality rating.
- ≥ 90% of high-risk use cases assessed using the standardized methodology.
- All new AI vendors in 2026 undergo due diligence prior to contract signature.
- AI policies and standards formally approved and communicated across key teams.

## 9. Assumptions and Dependencies
- Availability of subject matter experts (Legal, Security, Data, Business).
- Access to current system architecture, data maps, and vendor contracts.
- Executive support for enforcement and prioritization of remediation items.
- Alignment with broader enterprise risk management and compliance frameworks.
