# Workshop Room Capacity Review: 3-Room Model Alignment

**Project:** Microsoft Experience Centers Amsterdam  
**Review Date:** 2026-01-24  
**Purpose:** Verify if project specifications support the 3-room Balanced Model for workshop participation

---

## Executive Summary

The project summary mentions **"Workshop Areas"** (plural) but does **not explicitly specify** the number of dedicated rooms or their configurations. The current description is **compatible** with a 3-room model but requires **clarification and explicit confirmation** to ensure the Balanced Model can be fully implemented.

**Recommendation:** Update the project documentation to explicitly specify **3 dedicated workshop rooms** with the configurations outlined in the Balanced Model.

## Required 3-Room Model Specifications

### Room 1: Quick Demo Space
**Requirements:**
- **Capacity:** 12 people
- **Setup:** Flexible, quick turnover
- **Purpose:** 15-30 minute quick demos for walk-in retail customers
- **Equipment:** Minimal setup, quick reconfiguration
- **Sessions:** 8-10 per day

### Room 2: Standard Workshop Space
**Requirements:**
- **Capacity:** 15 people
- **Setup:** Standard tables, projectors
- **Purpose:** 60-minute standard workshops for both walk-ins and pre-registered customers
- **Equipment:** Tables, chairs, projectors, whiteboards, high-speed internet
- **Sessions:** 4-5 per day

### Room 3: Deep Dive Lab
**Requirements:**
- **Capacity:** 10 people
- **Setup:** Individual workstations
- **Purpose:** 90-120 minute hands-on labs for pre-registered customers
- **Equipment:** Individual workstations, coding environments, collaboration software
- **Sessions:** 2-3 per day

## Alternative Space Configurations

### Option 1: Single Flexible Space (Current Risk)
If only **one large workshop area** is planned:
- ❌ Cannot run concurrent workshops
- ❌ Limited to sequential sessions
- ❌ Cannot achieve 8-12 sessions per day across multiple rooms
- ❌ Would require **significantly higher daily capacity** per session to meet 10,000/year target

### Option 2: Two Rooms (Partial Solution)
If **two workshop areas** are planned:
- ⚠️ Can support some concurrent workshops
- ⚠️ May need to combine Quick Demo and Standard in one flexible space
- ⚠️ Lab space might need to be shared or scheduled separately
- ⚠️ Reduced flexibility compared to 3-room model

### Option 3: Three Rooms (Recommended - Balanced Model)
If **three dedicated workshop areas** are planned:
- ✅ Full support for concurrent workshops
- ✅ Optimal capacity utilization
- ✅ Clear separation of workshop types
- ✅ Meets Balanced Model requirements

## Recommendations

### 1. **Immediate Action: Clarify Workshop Room Specifications**

**Update Section 5.1** to explicitly state:

> "**Workshop Areas:** The Experience Center will include **three dedicated workshop spaces**, each optimized for different workshop types and customer engagement levels:
> 
> - **Quick Demo Space (Room 1):** Flexible space with capacity for 12 participants, designed for 15-30 minute quick demonstrations for walk-in retail customers. Features minimal setup requirements for quick turnover between sessions.
> 
> - **Standard Workshop Space (Room 2):** Standard workshop room with capacity for 15 participants, equipped with tables, projectors, whiteboards, and high-speed internet. Designed for 60-minute standard workshops accommodating both walk-in and pre-registered customers.
