## 1. Problem Statement & Opportunity

### 1.1 Problem

- **Traditional tech retail** often underperforms on engagement and conversion; customers cannot fully experience integrated hardware–software–cloud value.
- **Microsoft ecosystem** (Surface, M365, Azure) is under-explored in physical retail, despite strong demand for hands-on discovery and training.
- **Franchise expansion** lacks a standardized, data-driven blueprint for layout, customer flow, and operational QA.

### 1.2 Opportunity

- **Experiential retail** differentiates the brand and increases dwell time, attachment, and upsell (M365, Azure credits, accessories).
- **Workshop-led discovery** drives adoption and positions the shop as a learning hub.
- **Digital Twin** supports layout optimization, environmental efficiency, franchise QA, and virtual tours—enabling smarter rollout and operations.

---

## 2. Proposed Solution

A **Microsoft Experience Centers** (Amsterdam) concept comprising:

1. **Retail zone** – Surface hardware, accessories, Xbox, M365/Windows/Azure licensing.
2. **Workshop area** – 10–20 person capacity; M365, Power BI, Azure, creative/dev sessions.
3. **Product showcase** – Launch events, demos, Azure Media Services–powered content.
4. **Azure Solutions Demo Zone** – IoT, AI, Digital Twins, security demos; sensors + dashboards.
5. **Tech Bench** – Diagnostics, setup, migration, accessory testing.
6. **Digital Twin** – Shop layout, customer flow, product stations, environmental sensors; integrated with Azure IoT/Digital Twins.

## 4. Costs (Indicative)

*To be refined post–pilot location and fit-out scope.*

| Cost type | Pilot (single store) | Franchise rollout (per site) |
|-----------|----------------------|------------------------------|
| **Fit-out & build** | High (first-of-type) | Lower (standardised) |
| **Surface & demo hardware** | Significant (full range + workshop units) | Moderate (per blueprint) |
| **IoT/sensors & dashboards** | Moderate | Moderate (repeatable) |
| **Digital Twin build & integration** | Moderate (foundation) | Lower (reuse ontology + templates) |
| **Azure (IoT, Media, etc.)** | Recurring OpEx | Recurring per site |
| **Staff (retail + workshop leads)** | Recurring OpEx | Recurring per site |
| **Training & onboarding** | One-off + recurring | Per new franchise |

---

## 5. Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Impact | Mitigation |
|------|--------|------------|
| High demo hardware cost | CapEx overrun | Phased device rollout; prioritise hero products; partner funding where applicable. |
| Specialised staff requirements | OpEx, recruitment | Clear role specs; M365/Azure training; workshop playbooks. |
| Digital Twin accuracy & maintenance | DT value at risk | Start Level 0; define ownership; integrate with facility management. |
| Azure IoT dependency | Operational continuity | Design for resilience; fallbacks; SLA alignment with Azure. |

*See also [01-project-ideation §8](./01-project-ideation.md#8-risk--opportunity-analysis).*

## 6. Strategic Alignment

- **Microsoft Experience Centers** – Venue as experience-first destination.
- **Microsoft Innovation Hub** – Demos, Azure AI/IoT/Digital Twins, co-creation.
- **Customer Engagement Programs** – Workshops, training, adoption, ongoing engagement.
- **Ecosystem adoption** – Surface + M365 + Azure in one physical space.
- **Franchise growth** – Scalable, standardised, DT-enabled blueprint.
- **Azure-first** – IoT, Media, Digital Twins as differentiators.

---

## 7. Success Criteria (High Level)

- Pilot store operational within agreed timeline and budget.
- Digital Twin deployed with layout, flow, stations, and environmental sensors.
- Workshop programme launched with at least two curriculum tracks.
- Franchise blueprint (layout, operations, DT) documented and reusable.
- Positive stakeholder feedback from Retail Innovation & Experiential Commerce.

---

## 8. Options Considered

| Option | Description | Rationale |
|--------|-------------|-----------|
| **A. Full pilot (recommended)** | Pilot Microsoft Experience Centers Amsterdam + DT + workshops + Azure demos | Validates concept, DT, and franchise blueprint. |
| **B. Minimal pilot** | Retail + simple demos only | Lower cost, but weaker validation of workshops and DT. |
| **C. No physical pilot** | Virtual/concept only | No real-world validation of flow, sensors, or operations. |

**Recommendation:** Option A, with clear pilot scope and phase gates.

## 9. Approval & Next Steps

### 9.1 Recommended approval

Approve **project initiation** for Microsoft Experience Centers Amsterdam, including:

1. Stakeholder workshop for requirements refinement.  
2. Pilot location selection and fit-out scope.  
3. Level 0 Digital Twin modelling (layout, flow, stations, sensors).  
4. Development of detailed cost and ROI model for full business case sign-off.

1. **Stakeholder workshop** – Refine requirements, success criteria, and constraints.  
2. **Pilot location** – Select site; confirm floor space and lease terms.  
3. **Digital Twin Level 0** – Model layout, customer flow, product stations, environmental sensors (see [03-shop-layout-customer-flow-sensors](./03-shop-layout-customer-flow-sensors.md)).  
4. **Business case update** – Finalise CapEx/OpEx and ROI; secure full approval before pilot build.
