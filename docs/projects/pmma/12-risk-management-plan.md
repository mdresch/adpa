## 1. Executive Summary

### 1.1 Purpose

This Risk Management Plan (RMP) establishes a structured, proactive framework for identifying, analyzing, responding to, and monitoring threats and opportunities throughout PMMA's project lifecycle. Aligned with PMBOK 7's Uncertainty Performance Domain, this plan ensures PMMA delivers its vision with minimized disruptions and maximized value.

### 1.2 Key Objectives

| Objective | Description | Success Metric | Target Date |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|-------------|
| Proactive Risk Identification | Identify 100% of high-impact risks before they materialize | Risk Register completeness ≥95% | 2026-04-15 |
| Qualitative Risk Prioritization | Prioritize risks using 3x3 P-I Matrix | 100% of risks scored within 48 hours | 2026-04-20 |
| Response Strategy Execution | Implement strategies for 90% of high-impact risks | ≥90% response plans executed | 2026-06-30 |
| Opportunity Maximization | Exploit ≥3 high-value opportunities | ≥3 opportunities documented | 2026-08-30 |
| Reserve Management | Maintain Contingency Reserve at 10%, Management Reserve at 5% | Reserve utilization ≤80% | Ongoing |

## 2. Risk Management Approach

### 2.1 Methodology

PMMA follows a 6-step iterative process:

1. **Risk Identification**: Brainstorming, Delphi Technique, SWOT Analysis, Expert Judgment
2. **Qualitative Risk Analysis**: 3x3 Probability-Impact (P-I) Matrix
3. **Quantitative Risk Analysis**: Monte Carlo Simulation for high-impact risks
4. **Risk Response Planning**: Avoid, Mitigate, Transfer, Accept (threats); Exploit, Enhance, Share, Accept (opportunities)
5. **Risk Monitoring and Control**: Weekly reviews, monthly audits
6. **Risk Closure**: Formal closure when risk events are no longer possible

### 2.2 Risk Appetite and Thresholds

| Impact Level | Definition | Escalation Requirement |
|--------------|------------|------------------------|
| Critical (3) | Jeopardizes primary project objectives | Immediate Executive Escalation |
| High (2) | Impacts secondary objectives | Mitigation Strategy Required |
| Medium (1) | Localized disruption | Team-Level Management |
| Low (0) | Minimal impact | Monitoring Only |

### 2.3 Risk Appetite Matrix

| Risk Category | Appetite Level | Justification |
|---------------|----------------|---------------|
| Schedule | Moderate | Flexibility for AI/ML development |
| Cost | Low | Strict budget control required |
| Quality | Moderate | Tolerance for iterative improvements |
| Compliance | Low | Non-negotiable adherence |
| Stakeholder Satisfaction | High | Critical for adoption |

## 4. Risk Response Strategies

### 4.1 Negative Risks (Threats)

| Strategy | When to Use | Example |
|----------|-------------|---------|
| Avoid | Critical risks where impact is unacceptable | Conduct PoC before full AI/ML integration |
| Mitigate | High risks where probability or impact can be reduced | Engage backup vendor, weekly progress reviews |
| Transfer | High risks where third party can better manage | Fixed-price contract with cybersecurity vendor |
| Accept | Low risks or when response cost exceeds impact | Accept minor UX design delays |

### 4.2 Positive Risks (Opportunities)

| Strategy | Example |
|----------|---------|
| Exploit | Leverage Neo4j's latest features to enhance GKG performance by 20% |
| Enhance | Partner with PMI to co-develop scenario-based learning modules |
| Share | Joint go-to-market strategy with consulting firms |
| Accept | Accept integration opportunity if demand increases |
