## 1. Executive Summary

This Procurement Management Plan (PMP) establishes a structured framework for acquiring external goods and services required to deliver PMMA. Aligned with PMBOK 7 and the Delivery Performance Domain, this plan ensures procurement activities support PMMA's vision of creating a dynamic, data-driven "flight simulator" for project management.

### 1.1 Key Objectives

1. **Identify procurement needs** for all core components
2. **Establish vendor selection criteria** based on technical capability, cost, compliance, and scalability
3. **Define contract management processes** for performance monitoring and deliverable acceptance
4. **Mitigate procurement risks** through diversification and clear contract terms
5. **Ensure compliance** with organizational policies and PMBOK 7 standards

## 9. Procurement Risks

| Risk | Probability | Impact | Mitigation Strategy | Owner |
|------|-------------|--------|---------------------|-------|
| Vendor Non-Delivery | Medium | High | Penalties in contracts; maintain backup vendors | Procurement Manager |
| Cost Overruns | High | Medium | Competitive bidding; 10% contingency budget | Finance Lead |
| Compliance Violations | Low | High | Legal review of contracts; vendor audits | Legal & Compliance |
| Vendor Lock-In | Medium | Medium | Use open standards; modular contracts | Solution Architect |
| Integration Challenges | High | Medium | PoC testing before full procurement | IT Architect |
| Data Privacy Breaches | Low | High | Procure cybersecurity tools; vendor security assessments | CISO |

## 10. Procurement Closeout

### 10.1 Closeout Process

1. **Final Deliverable Review**: Comprehensive review against acceptance criteria
2. **Contract Completion**: Verify all obligations fulfilled
3. **Vendor Feedback**: Collect feedback on procurement process
4. **Lessons Learned**: Document successes, challenges, recommendations
5. **Contract Archiving**: Archive all contract documents
6. **Stakeholder Communication**: Inform stakeholders of closeout
