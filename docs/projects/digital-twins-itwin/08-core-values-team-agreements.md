## 1. Introduction

### 1.1 Purpose of the Document (PMBOK 7 Team Performance Domain)

This document establishes the Core Values and Team Agreements that will govern the conduct, decision-making, and cultural foundation of the ADPA Digital Twins iTwin IoT and Sensor Register project. Aligned with PMBOK 7's Team Performance Domain, these values are designed to foster a high-performing, psychologically safe, and collaborative environment that enables the team to deliver the project's strategic objectives.

The Team Performance Domain emphasizes the importance of culture, leadership, and shared ownership in driving project success. This document translates these principles into actionable behaviors that mitigate project risks while maximizing value delivery.

### 1.2 Project Context and the Need for Core Values

**Identified Risks That Core Values Address:**

1. **Communication Silos:** Misalignment between engineering and operations teams
2. **Technical Debt:** Poorly documented or rushed implementations
3. **Stakeholder Disengagement:** Low adoption rates among end-users
4. **Scope Creep:** Uncontrolled changes to requirements
5. **Vendor Misalignment:** Coordination challenges with Bentley Systems

**Alignment with ADPA's Organizational Values:**

- **Innovation:** Embracing cutting-edge digital technologies
- **Integrity:** Ensuring transparency and accountability
- **Collaboration:** Fostering cross-functional teamwork
- **Excellence:** Delivering high-quality outcomes

## 3. Team Agreements (Observable Behaviors)

### V-01: Ownership & Accountability

| Agreement | Enforcement/Monitoring |
|-----------|----------------------|
| **A-01:** Every team member is responsible for logging and tracking their own tasks. If a task is blocked, the owner must escalate within 1 hour. | Daily Stand-ups, Jira Workflow, PM monitors impediment log |
| **A-02:** We commit to documenting all decisions, assumptions, and technical implementations within 24 hours of a change. | Peer Review during sprint reviews, QA audits documentation |
| **A-03:** If unable to meet a commitment, proactively communicate the delay before the deadline. | PM tracks missed deadlines, retrospectives address root causes |

### V-02: Radical Transparency

| Agreement | Enforcement/Monitoring |
|-----------|----------------------|
| **A-04:** Critical issues, risks, or failures must be raised verbally within 1 hour and logged in the Risk Register within 4 hours. | PM monitors communication channels, Risk Register reviewed weekly |
| **A-05:** All project meetings will include a 5-minute "What Are We Missing?" segment to surface hidden risks. | Meeting Agendas include as standing item, Scrum Master facilitates |
| **A-06:** Share progress updates openly with all stakeholders through bi-weekly demos and monthly status reports. | Demo Sessions every 2 weeks, feedback incorporated into backlog |

### V-03: Collaborative Excellence

| Agreement | Enforcement/Monitoring |
|-----------|----------------------|
| **A-07:** All code, designs, and specifications must undergo peer review by at least one other team member. | Pull Requests require 1 approval, QA audits PRs for compliance |
| **A-08:** Hold bi-weekly cross-functional workshops to align engineering, operations, and IT teams. | Workshop Agendas prepared in advance, Action Items tracked in Jira |
| **A-09:** Actively seek and provide constructive feedback during sprint reviews and 1:1s. | Retrospectives include feedback segment, monthly 1:1s with PM |

### V-04: Focus on Value

| Agreement | Enforcement/Monitoring |
|-----------|----------------------|
| **A-10:** Every meeting must have a clear agenda, stated objective, and defined decision-maker. | Meeting Agendas distributed 24 hours in advance, Decision Log maintained |

| **A-12:** Conduct a "Value Check" at the start of each sprint to ensure alignment with Business Value Proposition. | Sprint Planning includes 10-minute Value Check, BA validates alignment |

| Agreement | Enforcement/Monitoring |

## 4. Conflict Resolution Process

### 4.1 Level 1: Direct Resolution

- **When to Use:** Minor disagreements between 2 team members
- **Process:**
  1. Schedule a private discussion within 24 hours
  2. Actively listen and seek mutually acceptable solution
  3. If unresolved, escalate to Level 2

### 4.2 Level 2: Mediation

- **When to Use:** Conflicts that cannot be resolved at Level 1 or involve multiple team members
- **Process:**
  1. Project Manager or Scrum Master facilitates a mediation session
  2. Guide discussion, ensure all voices are heard
  3. If unresolved, escalate to Level 3

### 4.3 Level 3: Escalation

- **When to Use:** Conflicts impacting project baseline (scope, timeline, budget)
- **Process:**
  1. PM escalates to Project Steering Committee
  2. Steering Committee reviews issue and makes final decision
  3. Decision is documented and communicated to the team

### 4.4 Conflict Resolution Principles

1. **Focus on the Problem, Not the Person:** Criticize ideas, not individuals
2. **Assume Positive Intent:** Approach conflicts believing all parties act in the project's best interest
3. **Seek Win-Win Solutions:** Prioritize outcomes that benefit the project and all stakeholders
4. **Document Decisions:** All conflict resolutions must be logged for future reference
