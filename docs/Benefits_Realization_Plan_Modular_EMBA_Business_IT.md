# Business Benefits Realization Plan (BRP)

## 1. Executive Summary

The **Modular Executive MBA in Business & IT** is a transformative educational program designed to bridge the critical gap between business leadership and technology-driven transformation. Accredited by **AMBA (London, UK)** and **EQUIS (Brussels, Belgium)**, this program empowers mid-to-senior professionals to enhance their strategic decision-making, leadership, and technological acumen through a flexible, modular format. The **Benefits Realization Plan (BRP)** outlines how the program will deliver measurable value to participants, employers, and the institution, ensuring alignment with strategic objectives and sustained competitive advantage.

This BRP translates the program’s outputs—such as accredited modules, industry-aligned curriculum, and flexible delivery—into tangible benefits, including **increased participant career advancement, enhanced organizational performance for employers, and strengthened institutional reputation**. By adopting a **PMBOK 7** framework, the plan ensures a structured approach to benefits identification, measurement, and realization, with clear ownership, timelines, and KPIs. The benefits are categorized into **Financial, Non-Financial, Strategic, and Dis-benefits**, with a focus on **immediate, mid-term, and long-term** value delivery.

Key stakeholders, including **participants, employers, faculty, accreditation bodies, and the Executive Education Board**, play critical roles in realizing these benefits. The plan also addresses dependencies, risks, and mitigation strategies to ensure successful outcomes. With a **3-year realization horizon**, the BRP serves as a living document to monitor progress, adapt to changing market dynamics, and maximize the program’s impact.

## 3. Benefit Profiles: Deep Dive

This section provides a detailed profile for the top 5 high-priority benefits, including KPIs, baselines, targets, and measurement mechanisms.

### 3.1 BEN-001: Program Revenue Growth
**Description:** Increased revenue from participant enrollment and corporate partnerships.
**Owner:** Director of Executive Education
**Type:** Financial

| Metric/KPI | Definition | Calculation Method |
|------------|------------|-------------------|
| Annual Revenue | Total revenue generated from participant enrollment and corporate partnerships. | Total revenue = (Number of participants × Tuition fee) + (Corporate bulk enrollments × Discounted fee) |

**Current Baseline:** Date: 2025-12-31 — Value: €5,000,000 (projected baseline for Year 1)

**Target Value:** Date: 2028-12-31 — Value: €8,000,000 (20% CAGR over 3 years)

**Measurement Source:** Financial reports from the institution’s ERP system (e.g., SAP).

**Frequency:** Annually

---

### 3.2 BEN-003: Participant Satisfaction and Engagement
**Description:** Enhanced participant satisfaction and engagement due to flexible, practice-based learning.
**Owner:** Academic Director
**Type:** Non-Financial

| Metric/KPI | Definition | Calculation Method |
|------------|------------|-------------------|
| Satisfaction Score | Average rating from post-module surveys (1-5 scale). | Satisfaction Score = Average rating from post-module surveys |
| Engagement Rate | Percentage of participants completing team-based case studies. | Engagement = % of participants completing team-based case studies |

**Current Baseline:** Date: 2025-12-31 — Value: Satisfaction: 4.2/5, Engagement: 85%

**Target Value:** Date: 2028-12-31 — Value: Satisfaction: 4.7/5, Engagement: 95%

**Measurement Source:** Post-module surveys and Learning Management System (LMS) analytics.

**Frequency:** Quarterly

---

### 3.3 BEN-004: Participant Career Advancement
**Description:** Improved participant career advancement and leadership capabilities through applied learning.
**Owner:** Module Leaders
**Type:** Non-Financial

| Metric/KPI | Definition | Calculation Method |
|------------|------------|-------------------|
| Career Growth Rate | Percentage of participants reporting career growth (e.g., promotions, salary increases) within 12 months of module completion. | % Career Growth = (Number of participants reporting advancement / Total participants) × 100 |

**Current Baseline:** Date: 2025-12-31 — Value: 60% (industry benchmark for Executive MBA programs)

**Target Value:** Date: 2028-12-31 — Value: 80%

**Measurement Source:** Annual participant follow-up surveys and LinkedIn profile analysis.

**Frequency:** Annually

---

### 3.4 BEN-005: Institutional Reputation and Competitiveness
**Description:** Strengthened institutional reputation and global competitiveness through AMBA/EQUIS accreditation.
**Owner:** Executive Education Board
**Type:** Strategic

| Metric/KPI | Definition | Calculation Method |
|------------|------------|-------------------|
| Reputation Score | Weighted average of QS/Financial Times rankings, employer survey scores, and participant NPS. | Reputation Score = Weighted average of rankings, employer surveys, and NPS |

**Current Baseline:** Date: 2025-12-31 — Value: Top 50 European Business Schools (QS Ranking)

**Target Value:** Date: 2028-12-31 — Value: Top 30 European Business Schools (QS Ranking)

**Measurement Source:** QS/Financial Times rankings, employer surveys, and participant NPS.

**Frequency:** Annually

---

### 3.5 BEN-007: Employer Organizational Performance
**Description:** Enhanced organizational performance for employers through upskilled leadership teams.
**Owner:** HR Directors
**Type:** Non-Financial

| Metric/KPI | Definition | Calculation Method |
|------------|------------|-------------------|
| Performance Improvement | Improvement in employer-reported organizational performance metrics (e.g., digital transformation success, innovation index). | Performance Improvement = Average % increase in employer-reported KPIs |

**Current Baseline:** Date: 2025-12-31 — Value: Baseline: 70% of employers report moderate improvement in digital transformation success.

**Target Value:** Date: 2028-12-31 — Value: 90% of employers report significant improvement in digital transformation success.

**Measurement Source:** Annual employer surveys and case study submissions.

**Frequency:** Annually

## 5. Dependencies and Risks

### 5.1 Dependencies

| Dependency | Impact on Benefits |
|------------|--------------------|
| Accreditation compliance with AMBA/EQUIS standards to maintain program legitimacy. | Failure to comply jeopardizes BEN-005 and BEN-001. |
| Corporate partnerships for bulk enrollments and employer-sponsored participants. | Critical for BEN-001 and BEN-006. |
| Faculty availability and expertise to deliver high-quality, market-driven modules. | Directly impacts BEN-003 and BEN-004. |
| Technology infrastructure (e.g., LMS, collaboration tools) to support flexible learning. | Essential for BEN-003 and BEN-007. |

---

### 5.2 Risks to Realization

| Risk Description | Mitigation Strategy |
|------------------|---------------------|
| Low participant enrollment due to market competition or economic downturns. | Implement targeted marketing campaigns, corporate partnerships, and flexible pricing models. |
| Participant dropout due to workload or misalignment with expectations. | Enhance participant onboarding, provide academic advising, and offer modular flexibility. |
| Failure to maintain AMBA/EQUIS accreditation due to non-compliance. | Establish a dedicated accreditation compliance team and conduct regular internal audits. |
| Faculty turnover or unavailability, leading to module delivery gaps. | Build a pipeline of adjunct faculty and industry experts, and implement succession planning. |
| Technological disruptions (e.g., LMS outages, cybersecurity breaches). | Invest in robust IT infrastructure, conduct regular security audits, and provide technical support. |

## 6. Dis-benefits

Dis-benefits are negative outcomes that the institution must accept or mitigate. The following table outlines potential dis-benefits and their impact on stakeholders.

| Impact | Affected Stakeholder |
|--------|----------------------|
| Participant attrition due to workload or misalignment with expectations, leading to lower enrollment and revenue. | Participants, Employers, Director of Executive Education |
| Market saturation due to competition from other Executive MBA programs, reducing program differentiation and enrollment. | Marketing Team, Business Development, Executive Education Board |
| Increased operational costs due to faculty turnover or unplanned module revisions, impacting profitability. | CFO / Finance Director, Academic Director |
| Reputation risk due to participant dissatisfaction or accreditation non-compliance, affecting institutional credibility. | Executive Education Board, Accreditation Bodies (AMBA/EQUIS) |
