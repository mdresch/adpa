## 1. Executive Summary

The **Solution Evaluation Plan** for the **Benefits Realization Management (BRM) Framework Implementation** establishes a structured approach to assess, validate, and optimize the BRM framework's effectiveness in delivering measurable business value. This plan aligns with PMBOK 7's **Value Delivery System** and BABOK v3's **Solution Evaluation** knowledge area, ensuring that the BRM framework meets organizational objectives, stakeholder expectations, and industry best practices.

Currently, only **37% of projects** successfully meet their original goals and business intent (PMI Pulse of the Profession, 2023), resulting in **$1.2 trillion wasted annually** on misaligned initiatives. This plan addresses this gap by:

- Defining **SMART evaluation criteria** to measure the BRM framework's performance.
- Establishing **governance structures** for continuous assessment and improvement.
- Implementing **metrics and KPIs** to track benefits realization, leakage, and time-to-benefit.
- Ensuring **stakeholder alignment** through structured engagement and reporting.

The evaluation will span the **entire project lifecycle**, from initiation to post-implementation review, with a focus on **strategic alignment, operational efficiency, and financial validation**. Key deliverables include the **Benefits Realization Policy, Benefits Realization Plan (BRP), Benefits Register, Benefits Tracking Dashboard, and Post-Implementation Review Report**.

## 2. Project Overview

### 2.1 Background

The **Benefits Realization Management (BRM) Framework Implementation** project was initiated to address a critical gap in the organization's ability to track, measure, and realize the benefits promised by its projects, programs, and portfolios. As outlined in the **Business Case** and **Project Charter**, the current lack of a structured BRM approach has led to:

- **Misaligned investments**: Projects are approved without clear linkage to strategic objectives.
- **Unrealized value**: Only **62% of planned benefits** are currently realized, with **38% leakage**.
- **Inefficient resource allocation**: Resources are spread thin across initiatives with unclear ROI.

The BRM framework will integrate benefits realization into every phase of the project lifecycle, from **initiation to post-implementation review**, ensuring that all initiatives deliver measurable value aligned with organizational strategy. The framework is designed to:

1. **Link to Organizational Strategy**: Map strategic objectives to portfolios, programs, and projects using a **Benefits Dependency Network (BDN)**.
2. **Establish Governance**: Create a **Benefits Realization Office (BRO)** or integrate BRM into the PMO, with defined roles and accountability.
3. **Integrate into Lifecycle**: Embed benefits realization into **initiation, planning, execution, closure, and post-implementation review** phases.
4. **Leverage Tools & Techniques**: Utilize **Balanced Scorecards, Benefits Tracking Dashboards, and Stakeholder Engagement Plans** for real-time monitoring and reporting.
5. **Measure Key Metrics**: Track **Benefits Realization Rate, Time-to-Benefit, and Benefits Leakage** to drive continuous improvement.
