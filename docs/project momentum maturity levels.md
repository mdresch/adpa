assessing the current maturity level of an organization in digital transformation requires a structured approach that blends governance checkpoints, stakeholder input, and measurable indicators. Here’s a practical framework you can apply to Project Momentum or any transformation initiative:

🔍 Steps to Assess Digital Transformation Maturity
1. Define the Maturity Dimensions
Break down the organization into key domains:
• 	Technology & Infrastructure – cloud adoption, system integration, automation.
• 	Data & Analytics – data governance, real-time insights, AI usage.
• 	Processes & Operations – workflow digitization, efficiency, scalability.
• 	Governance & Compliance – ICT governance frameworks, risk management, regulatory alignment.
• 	People & Culture – digital skills, adoption rates, change readiness.
• 	Customer Experience – personalization, omnichannel engagement, satisfaction metrics.

2. Develop Assessment Criteria
For each domain, define indicators across the 1–5 maturity scale:
• 	Level 1 (Ad Hoc) → Fragmented, reactive, siloed.
• 	Level 2 (Emerging) → Pilots, limited governance, early automation.
• 	Level 3 (Integrated) → Structured processes, cross-functional integration, formal governance.
• 	Level 4 (Optimized) → Enterprise-wide adoption, proactive compliance, advanced analytics.
• 	Level 5 (Transformative) → Continuous innovation, digital-first culture, governance as enabler.

3. Collect Evidence
• 	Surveys & Interviews → Gather perspectives from executives, managers, and employees.
• 	Document Review → Policies, governance frameworks, ICT roadmaps.
• 	System Audit → Technology stack, integration levels, automation coverage.
• 	Performance Metrics → KPIs like cycle times, NPS, adoption rates, compliance scores.

4. Score & Benchmark
• 	Assign scores (1–5) per domain.
• 	Use weighted averages if some domains are more strategic (e.g., governance for compliance-heavy industries).
• 	Benchmark against industry standards or peer organizations.

5. Identify Gaps & Roadmap
• 	Highlight domains stuck at lower maturity levels.
• 	Define governance checkpoints for progression (e.g., from reactive compliance → proactive compliance).
• 	Build a roadmap with clear milestones to move from current level to target level.

let’s design a maturity roadmap that shows how an organization can progress through the 5 governance maturity levels we drafted earlier, and then add a matrix (effort vs. cost) to prioritize initiatives.

🚀 Digital Transformation Governance Maturity Roadmap
Level 1 → Level 2 (Initial → Emerging)
• 	Actions:
• 	Conduct baseline digital maturity assessment.
• 	Define transformation vision and governance principles.
• 	Launch pilot projects in key domains (HR, finance, customer).
• 	Governance checkpoint: Executive sponsorship and project-level governance.

Level 2 → Level 3 (Emerging → Integrated)
• 	Actions:
• 	Build enterprise-wide roadmap with milestones.
• 	Standardize processes and integrate cross-functional systems.
• 	Establish governance committees and risk management frameworks.
• 	Governance checkpoint: Formalized governance structures and compliance monitoring.

Level 3 → Level 4 (Integrated → Optimized)
• 	Actions:
• 	Scale successful pilots across the enterprise.
• 	Deploy advanced analytics and AI-driven dashboards.
• 	Embed change management and workforce upskilling programs.
• 	Governance checkpoint: Proactive compliance and adaptive governance policies.

Level 4 → Level 5 (Optimized → Transformative)
• 	Actions:
• 	Institutionalize continuous improvement cycles.
• 	Foster ecosystem partnerships (OEMs, regulators, tech providers).
• 	Embed sustainability and resilience into digital strategy.
• 	Governance checkpoint: Governance as a strategic enabler of innovation.
