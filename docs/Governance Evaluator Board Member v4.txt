Governance Evaluator Board Member
Audits compliance and rules enforcement against stated architecture frameworks.
Findings
The template guidance demonstrates exceptional alignment with BABOK v3 principles, particularly within the Strategy Analysis and Solution Evaluation Knowledge Areas.
Strong emphasis on 'Determine Value' is evident through explicit requirements for quantified benefits, robust financial analysis (ROI, NPV, Payback Period with method clarification), and detailed comparison of solution options.
The 'Root Cause Analysis' requirement directly supports BABOK's 'Analyze Current State' task, ensuring a deep understanding of the problem.
The 'Transition State' requirement directly addresses BABOK's 'Define Change Strategy' and the pathway to the 'Future State'.
Traceability is excellently enforced through the 'Actionable Requirements' section, mandating measurable Success Criteria linked to the initial 'Business Need' and including baseline metrics.
The 'Sensitivity Analysis' requirement effectively integrates risk assessment related to financial viability, aligning with BABOK's 'Assess Risks' task.
High standards for professional communication, accuracy, and explicit population of roles (Sponsor, Project Manager) contribute to the overall rigor and usability of the template.
The integration of PMBOK principles (e.g., 'Outcomes over Outputs', 'Value Delivery') complements the BABOK focus without diluting its core tenets, showing a holistic approach to project justification.
Compliance Gaps

Remediation Guidelines
Consider adding an explicit requirement for a high-level Stakeholder Analysis section within the Business Case, outlining key stakeholders, their interests, and potential impact/support for the proposed change. This aligns with BABOK's Elicitation and Collaboration knowledge area (Plan Stakeholder Engagement) and Strategy Analysis (Assess Risks - Stakeholder Impact Analysis).
Suggest including a section to assess the organization's current capabilities, resources, and readiness to successfully implement the proposed change and sustain the future state. This would strengthen alignment with BABOK's 'Analyze Current State' and 'Define Future State' tasks by considering internal capacities for change.
While solution integrity is covered by pros/cons/costs, it could be enhanced by a high-level mention of critical non-functional requirements (e.g., security, performance, scalability) that significantly impact the value proposition or solution feasibility, where applicable.
Counterfactual Challenger Board Member
Stresses template instructions, loops, and vagueness to prevent AI hallucinations.
Findings
The template implicitly assumes the user will provide comprehensive, quantitative, and perfectly structured data for all required sections (e.g., financial figures, specific root causes, multiple solution options, baseline metrics). If not, the AI is implicitly forced to hallucinate or generate generic content.
Unrealistic expectation of LLM accuracy and verifiability for complex, multi-step financial calculations (NPV, ROI, Sensitivity Analysis). LLMs are prone to arithmetic errors and logical inconsistencies in numerical tasks.
Ambiguous and potentially conflicting instruction regarding 'All dates must be current,' which could lead to overwriting user-provided historical or context-specific project dates.
Lack of explicit guidance on how the AI should handle missing critical information from user input (e.g., Business Sponsor/Project Manager names, discount rate, specific quantitative benefits, validation methods). This creates a loophole for AI to either hallucinate values or fail to meet requirements.
Mandating a minimum number of pros/cons (at least 3 for each option) without ensuring the user provides sufficient detail for multiple distinct options, risking generic, irrelevant, or hallucinated content.
The directive to 'Express all benefits... as projected monetary values' pushes the AI to monetize qualitative benefits without a specified methodology or user input, leading to potential high-severity financial hallucination.
The incomplete instruction 'Paragraphs & Outlines: []' in the Response Format section is vague and provides no actionable guidance.
Logical Vulnerabilities
Location	Description	Severity
System Prompt / Guidance: 'Ensure all outputs are accurate, compliant, and consistent.' & 'Core Competencies & Logic Requirements: Financial Rigor (NPV Logic): ...Ensure ALL financial calculations are accurate and verifiable.'	The demand for 'accurate' and 'verifiable' financial calculations by an LLM is a fundamental logical vulnerability. LLMs generate text based on patterns and probabilities, not deterministic computation. While they can produce formulas and apply them, the actual arithmetic can be flawed, leading to plausible but incorrect numbers (hallucination) that undermine the 'fiscally responsible' objective.	high
💡 Suggested Fix: Add a clear disclaimer about the AI's role in providing *estimates* and the necessity of human verification for all financial data. Instruct the AI to show step-by-step calculations for transparency.
System Prompt / Guidance: 'All dates must be current.'	This instruction is logically inconsistent with typical project management, which involves past start dates, future milestones, and contextual timelines. Enforcing 'current' dates universally could lead to the AI altering project-specific dates provided by the user, thereby corrupting the project's historical context or future plan.	medium
💡 Suggested Fix: Clarify the instruction: 'Ensure all *future projection dates* are relative to the current generation date. If the user provides specific historical or projected dates, prioritize those and state their context clearly.'
System Prompt / Guidance: 'Business Sponsor and Project Manager names must be explicitly populated, not TBD.' & 'Goal: ...The justification must be supported by a robust, quantitative Cost-Benefit Analysis... evaluating proposed solutions based on measurable returns (ROI, NPV, Payback Period).'	The prompt mandates the inclusion of specific, non-TBD information (names) and quantitative analysis (financial metrics) but fails to provide instructions for the AI on how to proceed if the user input is incomplete (e.g., missing names, no discount rate, qualitative-only benefits). This forces the AI to either make unsupported assumptions, hallucinate data, or fail to meet the prompt's requirements.	high
💡 Suggested Fix: Instruct the AI to explicitly prompt the user for missing mandatory information or to clearly state any assumptions it makes (e.g., 'Assumed discount rate: X% (user input not provided)').
Core Competencies & BABOK V3 Alignment: 'Solution Integrity: ...Provide at least 3 pros and 3 cons for each proposed option.'	This instruction imposes a quantitative minimum (3 pros/cons) without guaranteeing sufficient or distinct user input for multiple options. If the user provides limited details or only one option, the AI might generate generic, redundant, or hallucinated pros/cons simply to meet the count, thereby diminishing the 'objective analysis' and 'solution integrity.'	medium
💡 Suggested Fix: Instruct the AI to prioritize quality and relevance. If it cannot generate 3 distinct and specific pros/cons based on user input, it should state this limitation and either ask for more detail or clearly label any generated points as generalized.
Response Format: 'Paragraphs & Outlines: []'	This is an incomplete and non-actionable instruction. It's a placeholder that offers no guidance to the AI or the human reviewer, potentially causing confusion or being ignored entirely, indicating a flaw in the prompt's design.	low
💡 Suggested Fix: Remove this line or complete it with specific instructions on paragraph structure or outline expectations.
Prompt Sharpening Guidelines
Explicitly instruct the AI to *ask clarifying questions* or *prompt the user for specific missing data* when critical information (e.g., financial inputs, names, specific project details, discount rate, baseline metrics) is absent or insufficient for a robust analysis.
Add a prominent disclaimer to the System Prompt, and potentially to the generated Business Case, stating that 'AI-generated financial calculations are estimates and require independent human verification for accuracy and verifiability.'
Clarify the 'All dates must be current' instruction: 'Ensure all *future projection dates* are relative to the current generation date. If the user provides specific historical or projected dates, prioritize those, clearly stating their context rather than overwriting them.'
For mandatory fields like Business Sponsor and Project Manager names, instruct the AI: 'If these names are not provided by the user, explicitly state 'Business Sponsor: [Awaiting User Input]' and 'Project Manager: [Awaiting User Input]' instead of TBD or hallucinating names.'
Refine the 'Quantified Value' directive: 'If the user provides qualitative benefits, either explicitly *ask the user for their preferred quantification methodology and values* or *propose a methodology and clearly state all assumptions made* to monetize them.'
For requirements like 'minimum 3 pros and 3 cons' or 'minimum 5 sentences,' instruct the AI to prioritize *relevance and specificity* based on user input. If sufficient detail isn't available, the AI should clearly state that certain points are generalized or derived from common industry practices due to limited user input.
Provide a full, standard outline or Table of Contents for the Business Case document to ensure consistent structure and completeness.
Remove or complete the 'Paragraphs & Outlines: []' instruction in the Response Format section.
